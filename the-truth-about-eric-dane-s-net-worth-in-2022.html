He May Best Be Known For His TV Work, But Eric Dane Is No Stranger To Films

It may have been purely coincidental, but Dane ended up landing the part of Multiple Man in X-Men: The Last Stand around the same time that he got cast as Mark Sloan. Soon after, the actor also starred in the adventure drama Open Water 2: Adrift, which is based on true events.

Shortly after, Dane also joined the cast of the comedy-drama Marley & Me, which was headlined by Jennifer Aniston and Owen Wilson. In the film, the actor played Wilson’s single friend Sebastian.

“Sebastian leads what seems to be an ideal single man’s lifestyle,” the actor explained during an interview with Emanuel Levy. “Sebastian is always happy to listen to John and give him advice – as long as a beautiful woman and potential conquest doesn’t walk by to distract them.”

Meanwhile, Dane also starred in the ensemble rom-com Valentine’s Day where he played an NFL player who is in a relationship with Bradley Cooper. The two actors even shared a kissing scene, which made their co-star, Jessica Alba, a tad “jealous.”

“In Valentine's Day when Bradley Cooper had his kissing scene with another actor... I was a bit like, 'Man! I could have been on that!’” the actress confessed while on The Kyle and Jackie O show for Australian radio channel 2DayFm.

Eric Dane Also Landed Some Major Series Roles After ‘Grey’s Anatomy’

After working on a medical drama for years, Dane traded the OR for a battleship in the TNT drama The Last Ship. As it turns out, the project came at just the right time for the actor.

“It was three weeks after I’d wrapped Grey’s when this all happened, and it just was something I couldn’t pass up,” Dane told Showbiz Junkies.

“It just kind of unfolded perfectly and was just kind of the perfect storm of circumstances that led me here.”

The Last Ship was also produced by Michael Bay and for Dane, that was one of the show’s main selling points.

“Well, first of all, it was a Michael Bay show, and the action genre has always been intriguing to me,” the actor explained.

“I read the script and aside from it being whatever it is, virus, action, drama, no drama, it’s some of the best writing I’ve ever read.”

At the same time, showrunner Hank Steinberg also told The Hollywood Reporter that Bay was responsible for “getting Eric Dane to agree to do the pilot.”

“Of course, it doesn’t hurt to have Eric Dane if you’re looking for female viewership,” he later added. “That takes care of a lot of it off the bat.” The Last Ship went on for five seasons.

Meanwhile, more recently, Dane joined the cast of the HBO hit drama Euphoria as Nate’s (Jacob Elordi) father, Cal Jacobs. Early on, the actor made it clear that he was fully committed to portraying a married man harboring an explosive secret, even when that involved a degree of nudity.

“I’m willing to do anything that’s critical to the story and crucial to creating a very real and truthful feel to how the story is gonna go down,” Dane told Entertainment Weekly.

At one point, the actor even filmed a scene with frontal nudity as it was revealed that his character assaulted a 17-year-old trans girl.

“I just don’t see how you shoot a scene like that without showing nudity,” the actor explained. “And, you know, it kind of matches the stakes. The stakes are so high, you can’t hold anything back, really.”
