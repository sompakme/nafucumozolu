The Life and Journey of Iris Apfel

Iris Apfel, born Iris Barrel, is a fashion icon and businesswoman who was born on August 29, 1921, in Astoria, Queens, New York. She studied art history at NYU and attended art school at the University of Wisconsin. Apfel’s career started as a copywriter for Women’s Wear Daily before she ventured into the fashion and design industry. She married Carl Apfel in 1948, and they ran their textile firm, Old World Weavers, for 42 years. The couple’s work in textiles earned them contracts with nine White House presidents. Apfel’s journey is a testament to her passion, creativity, and entrepreneurial spirit.

At a young age, Iris Apfel showed a keen interest in fashion and art. Her studies in art history provided her with a strong foundation in aesthetics and a deep appreciation for design. This knowledge, combined with her innate sense of style, set her on a path towards becoming a fashion icon.

Throughout her career, Apfel has been recognized for her unique and eclectic style, which blends vintage and contemporary pieces in unexpected ways. Her bold fashion choices and larger-than-life accessories have become her signature, and she has become known as the “rare bird of fashion.”

Early Career and Textile Business

Before making a name for herself in the fashion industry, Apfel started her career as a copywriter for Women’s Wear Daily. This experience gave her insights into the fashion world and helped her develop her own personal style.

In 1950, Apfel and her husband, Carl Apfel, founded their textile firm, Old World Weavers. The company specialized in reproducing antique textiles and creating custom fabrics for high-profile clients, including the White House and various museums. Their exceptional work in the textile industry earned them contracts with nine US presidents and garnered recognition for their contributions to interior design.
