5 XFL Compensation

Earnings From XFL: Negative $60 Million

  • Dwayne Johnson projects the 2024 season of the XFL will bring in $100 million in revenue

Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson and his business partners, Dany Garcia and RedBird Capital, purchased the XFL (Xtreme Football League) in August 2020. The XFL is a professional American football league that was initially launched in 2001 and has had multiple attempts at revival.

The exact financial terms of the XFL purchase were not publicly disclosed. However, it was reported that the group led by Dwayne Johnson acquired the league for a total purchase price of around $15 million. This acquisition was made after the XFL filed for bankruptcy earlier in 2020.

It's important to note that the initial XFL revival in 2020 faced significant challenges due to the COVID-19 pandemic, which resulted in the suspension of the season after just five weeks of play. Despite the financial and operational setbacks, the purchase of the XFL presented an opportunity for Dwayne Johnson and his partners to potentially reshape and revitalize the league for future seasons.

Since the acquisition, Dwayne Johnson has been actively involved in efforts to bring back the XFL and explore ways to improve the league's structure and sustainability, one of those being his 2023 partnership with the NFL.

According to Variety, the partnership does not involve games or teams specifically but is an agreement to work together in studying the game of football and collaborating on forward-thinking physical and mental fitness programs for players.

To date, the XFL has not proven to be a successful venture for Johnson. But, given his track record, Johnson will make the league a success. It is this track record that has helped him attract investors to bring Johnson's vision to life and why he is projecting the league will make $100 million in revenue in 2024, with ESPN holding media rights through 2027.
